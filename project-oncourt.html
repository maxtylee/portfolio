OnCourt: Athletic Training Tracker

🔷 Context / Problem:
Tennis players struggle to quantify wrist motion during training. OnCourt addresses the need for a lightweight, wearable tracker to improve swing consistency in real-time.

My Role:
I led the mechanical design, prototyping, and electronics integration for the wearable device.

Solution Summary:
Designed a wrist-mounted enclosure for ESP32 with IMU and OLED display, providing visual feedback and wireless data capture via WiFi/Bluetooth.

Technical Highlights:

  • 🌿 CAD modelled ergonomic case in Fusion 360 for compact form
  • ✏️ Used FDM 3D printing for physical iterations and testing
  • 🔢 Integrated MPU6050 IMU and SSD1306 OLED on ESP32 board
  • 🚀 Implemented onboard motion tracking and basic visualization

Results / Key Outcomes:

  • ✔️ Final form factor weighs <10g and fits within 1.5" x 1.5"
  • 📊 Functional prototype demoed to 100+ attendees at Expo Day
  • 📊 Firmware tested for 30+ swings with reliable feedback loop
